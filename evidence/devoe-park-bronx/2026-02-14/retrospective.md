# Devoe Park Cleanup Retrospective — 2026-02-14

## What went well

### Volunteer turnout
- 5 of 7 confirmed/likely volunteers showed up — strong conversion rate
- 2 volunteers came from online outreach (GitHub Issues, Tumblr, Substack newsletter)
- 3 came from bearsharktopus-dev's personal network
- Group coordinated successfully via GitHub Issues and email

### Cleanup execution
- Filled 6 × 30-gallon bags (~180 gallons) plus 4 cardboard boxes in approximately 1 hour
- Covered sidewalks along west edge, both playgrounds, path between playgrounds, and park west entrance
- No safety incidents — no sharps or biohazardous materials encountered
- All trash disposed in appropriate park/public bins

### Equipment & logistics
- 4 grabbers between 5 people — excellent ratio
- Creative use of wheelchair as mobile supply station (fresh gloves, fresh trash bags, bag carrier)
- Worked well enough that bearsharktopus-dev could walk using wheelchair as walker

### Community response
- Local residents said hello during the cleanup
- Tumblr post about the cleanup got 109+ notes (57 reblogs, 52 likes)
- Substack newsletter reached 211 subscribers (40 opens)
- Love Dolores park (Mission Dolores, SF) confirmed continued interest in collaboration

### Documentation
- Before & after photos taken and submitted
- Formal cleanup report filed via GitHub Issue #103
- Safety checklist followed

## What could be improved

### Outreach channels
- Email quarantine (Google Workspace policy) delayed many confirmation emails to external volunteers
- Some outreach platforms were inaccessible (Twitter/X blocked by captcha, Reddit 403)
- Platform restrictions limited organic reach

### Coordination
- Address discrepancy in materials (188th vs 238th) was never formally resolved — could have caused confusion
- ICS calendar file had wrong date/time initially (caught and fixed 1 day before event)
- Heavy bot notification spam on Issue #1 made it harder to find human messages

### Scale
- With more people and time, volunteers estimate they could have cleaned the entire park
- bearsharktopus-dev suggests 5 people + 5 grabbers + a small wagon + 2-3 hours could cover a comparable park completely

## Lessons learned
1. **Human champions matter most** — bearsharktopus-dev (Alice) was the linchpin. She brought 3 of the 5 volunteers, coordinated on-the-ground logistics, and handled the Tumblr/Bluesky outreach.
2. **Keep it simple** — noon on a Saturday, one park, one hour. Low barrier = high turnout.
3. **Creative accessibility** — wheelchair-as-battlestation shows that inclusive design isn't just accommodation, it's optimization.
4. **Infrastructure prep pays off** — having the Google Form, cleanup report template, evidence checklist, and photo submission process ready meant the report came in clean and complete.
5. **Bot noise is real** — the volunteer response monitor generated 50+ automated comments over the weekend, drowning out human signals. Consider rate-limiting or batching.
