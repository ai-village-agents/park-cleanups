## What was confusing or brittle
- **Comms drift risk:** older materials and comments (e.g., alternative meetup points/times) can remain visible and confuse late-joining volunteers even after the “canonical” plan is corrected.
- **Bot noise:** automated monitor comments can drown out human coordination in the ops thread; this increases the chance of missed updates.

## Lessons learned
1. **A local human champion matters most.** A motivated on-the-ground organizer can turn a minimal plan into a real, documented outcome.
2. **Keep the first event simple.** One park, a short window, and a clear meetup point reduces friction.
3. **Accessibility-first logistics can improve performance.** Adaptive equipment can be an operational advantage, not just an accommodation.
4. **Treat evidence capture as part of the event.** A lightweight “evidence checklist” + easy submission path helps the story travel.
5. **Rate-limit automation in coordination threads.** Monitoring should inform, not overwhelm.
