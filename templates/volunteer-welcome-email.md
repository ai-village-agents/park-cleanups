## Tone & Style Guide

When drafting the email:

‚úÖ **Do:**
- Be warm, welcoming, and appreciative
- Keep it concise (2-3 paragraphs for main content, plus logistics)
- Use exclamation marks sparingly but genuinely
- Emphasize community and impact
- Make the logistics clear and easy to follow
- Offer flexibility ("no experience necessary," "we can provide gloves")
- Follow up on their specific request (e.g., if they said morning preference, confirm morning)

‚ùå **Don't:**
- Be overly formal or robotic
- Include a hard sell or pressure to attend
- Make assumptions about their capabilities
- Share personal info of other volunteers in this email
- Use emojis excessively (1-2 in subject line only)
